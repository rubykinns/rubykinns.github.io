About the Project

During my time at HAII Corporation in Seoul, I worked as a UX researcher and designer on SAEMI — a software-based AI chatbot companion designed to support cognitive health, primarily targeting Alzheimer's disease prevention and other mental illnesses through cognitive training games and guided conversation.

Unlike traditional drugs or medical devices, SAEMI interacts directly with users as a compassionate AI companion — making it a standout product in the digital therapeutics space. My work spanned branding, visual UX/UI design, and marketing.

Branding

03 · Branding

I created a new branding identity for the SAEMI application under the name Alzguard 2.0.

Naming Rationale

Alzheimer's disease + Guard = Alzguard. The name communicates the core mission: protecting users from cognitive decline through a proactive, AI-driven companion — not a passive tool.

Alzguard 2.0 is the digital therapeutics platform powering SAEMI — a software-based AI chatbot that aims to prevent Alzheimer's disease and other mental illnesses through cognitive training games and conversation. The key differentiator: while drugs and medical devices don't interact with users, SAEMI does.

Marketing

04 · Marketing

To grow SAEMI's user base, I took on a multifaceted marketing role — producing video content, managing the official blog, and contributing to TV commercial efforts.

  • Video Production — Filmed and edited promotional videos for SAEMI
  • Blog Management — Published regular posts covering digital therapeutics, mental illness, mental healthcare, and SAEMI updates
  • TV Commercials — Contributed to broadcast advertising campaigns
Impact

Due to marketing efforts, SAEMI saw a 200% user increase from the time I joined the team to the end of my tenure — demonstrating the direct business impact of integrated UX and marketing work.

Reflection

  • Cross-functional collaboration is a skill in itself. Working alongside developers, designers, and medical advisors taught me how to translate between technical constraints and user-centered goals.
  • Brand identity shapes user trust. In digital therapeutics, trust is the product. The Alzguard 2.0 rebrand reinforced that every visual decision — from color to naming — carries clinical and emotional weight.
  • Marketing and UX are not separate. Seeing a 200% user increase as a direct result of integrated content and design work showed me that the user journey doesn't start at the product — it starts at awareness.
